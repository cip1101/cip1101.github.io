HAVE YOU TRIED EATING COCHINILLO?

Cochinillo is a roasted sucking pig, it has less fat and is tender enough that even a plate can cut through it. Though a Cochinillo is smaller, the skin is crispy and is packed with amazing flavors.

WHERE TO ORDER COCHINILLO IN MANILA?

Home celebrations will never be complete without some delicious food. For Filipinos, it is truly a celebration if there is Lechon present. But since our celebrations are smaller, Cochinillo is probably a better option. It’s smaller, more affordable and perfect for our intimate home celebrations. Here, we've listed 3 shops where you can find the best Cochinillo in Manila.


1. Secret Kitchen

Fancy a celebrity cooking your meal? Secret Kitchen is Marvin Agustin’s personal project.
The whole idea came from the actor/slash/chef/businessman’s ambition to replicate a roast suckling pig that he had in Spain. It took him 6 years, but the result, affectionately called “cochi,” is a beautiful little pig slow-cooked for 4 hours in Agustin’s patented mix of herbs and spices.
PRICELIST:
Starts at P6,999-P7,999
HOW TO ORDER?
DM @bysecretkitchen on Instagram


2. Señor Cochinillo

This newbie was launched at the end of November, just in time for the holidays. Based in Marikina, it’s still currently being sold at a special introductory price (grab that deal!). It’s classic, perfectly roasted to a glossy brown with flavorful tender meat. It’s perfect with either of their pastas or paellas.
PRICELIST:
Php 5,900
HOW TO ORDER?
DM @senorcochinillo on Instagram


3. The Murang Cochinillo

It seems that roasting bovines was on everyone’s minds in the past few months. Another business launched during the quarantine, these apparently affordable suckling pigs come as an entire set. Apart from the 4 to 5 kilogram pig, it also comes with a duo of sauces and four kinds of Filipino desserts.
PRICELIST:
Price: P8,888;
HOW TO ORDER?
DM @cochinilloandbeyond on Instagram
