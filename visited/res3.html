What is so special about Mendokoro Ramenba?

Those familiar with Ramen Yushoken can think of Mendokoro Ramenba as its trimmed-down sibling. The menu is more concise, leaving only ramen and gyoza as its perfect complement. A safe bet for newcomers is the flavorful and surprisingly bold Shoyu (P410), though regulars swear by the Miso (P460). The chasu or pork slices are succulent and smoky and are the right size to be hefty without being too filling. After all, you're here for the main event: The creamy tonkotsu broth blending with miso, which is at turns tangy and spicy. The noodles, too, are a standout, flaunting the perfect balance of firm and chewy
